Pablo Picasso

  • 1881 - Born in Málaga, Spain and was baptized Pablo Diego José Francisco de Paula Juan Nepomuceno María de los Remedios Cipriano de la Santísima Trinidad Ruiz y Picasso in the Catholic Church, though he later became an atheist.
  • 1888 - Began receiving instruction in drawing and oil painting from his father who was a painter that specialized in naturalistic depictions of animals.
  • 1895 - Admitted to advance classes at the Barcelona School of Fine Arts at 13 years of age.
  • 1897 - His father send him to Madrid's Real Academia de Bellas Artes de San Fernando, which was one of Spain's foremost art school. However he bristled at the formal instruction and left the school shortly after enrollment. He instead spend much of his time being inspired by the exhibits of other artists in the Prado Museum. It was during this time that the realism of his work began the show Symbolist influences.
  • 1900 - Makes his first trip to Paris, which was the art capitol of Europe at the time. There he met a journalist and poet name Max Jacob, who help Picasso learn the French language and its literature.
  • 1901-1904 - Picasso's Blue Period. This period is characterized by the use of blue and blue-green shades in his paintings with the occasional of other colors. These paintings were somber and often depicted gaunt mothers with children, prostitutes, and beggars.
